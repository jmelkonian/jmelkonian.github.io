About

I am a rising sixth year Public Policy PhD student (ABD) at Harvard Kennedy School. My research focuses on the impacts of international migration on economic development, with a focus on labor.

I have taught two sophomore tutorials at Harvard College on the economics of migration in the Middle East and North Africa.

Prior to Harvard, I double majored in economics and international relations at UC Davis (class of 2019), and worked at the Chicago Federal Reserve Bank for two years as a research assistant.

I am an applied microeconomist studying the economics of migration, development, and labor. My research focuses on the economic implications of migration for sending regions, investigating how migration affects local labor markets and development outcomes in the places migrants leave.
