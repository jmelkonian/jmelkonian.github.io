Job Market Paper

Shock Propagation through Migrant Networks:
Evidence from Subnational Migration in the Spain-Ecuador Migration Corridor

I study the impact of labor market shocks in Spain on economic development in Ecuador during the Global Financial Crisis and Eurozone Debt Crisis. I find Spanish shocks propagate to Ecuador, with Ecuadorian province exposure to Spanish labor market downturns significantly associated with increases in return migration.

Emigration by Exodus: Minority Emigration and Economic Development in North African Decolonization

I investigate the near-universal emigration of European colonists and Sephardic Jewish minorities surrounding North African decolonization as natural experiments related to the impact of emigration on development, leveraging archival censuses and contemporary survey and census microdata.
